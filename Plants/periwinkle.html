Periwinkle (Catharanthus roseus)

Periwinkle, also known as Madagascar periwinkle or vinca, is a resilient ornamental plant with vibrant pink, purple, or white flowers. It’s also a powerful medicinal herb used in modern and traditional medicine.
Plant Attributes & Uses

Scientific Classification

Family: Apocynaceae
Genus: Catharanthus
Species: C. roseus

Global Spread

Native to Madagascar, now grown in tropical and subtropical regions worldwide as an ornamental and medicinal plant.

Preferred Climate

Thrives in warm climates with full sun to partial shade; drought-tolerant once established.

Medicinal Uses

Source of vincristine and vinblastine, used in cancer treatment; also supports wound healing and diabetes management.

Traditional Uses

Leaves and extracts used in herbal medicine for blood sugar control and immune support.

Cosmetic Benefits

Sometimes used in skin preparations for its astringent and antioxidant effects.

Garden Information

Hardiness: Zones 9–11
Climate Zones: Tropical, Subtropical
Exposure: Full Sun to Partial Shade
Soil Type: Well-drained, moderately fertile soil
Water Needs: Moderate; avoid waterlogging
