Lemongrass (Cymbopogon citratus)

Lemongrass is a tall, tropical grass with a citrus scent, widely used in culinary dishes, herbal remedies, and essential oils for its fresh aroma and health benefits.
Plant Attributes & Uses

Scientific Classification

Family: Poaceae
Genus: Cymbopogon
Species: C. citratus

Global Spread

Native to Southeast Asia, now grown in tropical and subtropical regions worldwide.

Preferred Climate

Warm, humid climates with full sun and well-drained soil; frost sensitive.

Medicinal Uses

Aids digestion, reduces fever, relieves stress, and has antibacterial properties.

Traditional Uses

Common in teas, infusions, and poultices; used for its calming and purifying qualities.

Cosmetic Benefits

Found in soaps, shampoos, and oils for its refreshing scent and antimicrobial effects.
