Tulsi Plant (Ocimum tenuiflorum)

About the Tulsi Plant

Location: Native Range: Tulsi is native to the Indian subcontinent and Southeast Asia.

Common Cultivation: It is widely cultivated in tropical and subtropical regions around the world, especially in India, where it is often found in home gardens and temples.

Habitat: Tulsi thrives in warm, sunny locations with well-drained soil. It can grow in various soil types, though it prefers fertile, loamy soil.

History: Cultural Significance: Tulsi is highly revered in Hindu culture and is often considered a sacred plant. It is associated with the goddess Tulsi and is worshipped in many Hindu households.

Historical Use: It has been used for thousands of years in traditional Indian medicine (Ayurveda) for its therapeutic properties.

Symbolism: In Hindu mythology, Tulsi represents purity and is considered a protector of the home. It is often planted near temples and homes to ward off negative energies.

Medical Uses: Boosts immunity, reduces stress, antibacterial.
