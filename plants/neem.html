Neem Plant (Azadirachta indica)

About the Neem Plant

Location: Native Region: Neem is native to the Indian subcontinent, particularly India, Pakistan, Nepal, Bangladesh, and Sri Lanka.

Global Spread: Today, it is grown in tropical and subtropical regions worldwide, including Southeast Asia, Africa, the Caribbean, Central and South America.

Preferred Climate: Thrives in hot, arid regions with minimal rainfall. It can survive in areas with temperatures up to 49°C (120°F) and tolerates dry conditions.

History: Ancient Usage: Neem has been used in Indian Ayurveda for over 4,000 years. It is often referred to as the "Village Pharmacy" due to its extensive medicinal properties.

Religious and Cultural Significance: Neem is considered sacred in Hindu culture. Its leaves and branches are often used in traditional ceremonies, festivals, and home altars.

Traditional Applications: Historically, neem was used for treating skin disorders, infections, fevers, and even as a natural pesticide in agriculture.

Ayurvedic Texts: Ancient Ayurvedic texts describe Neem as a plant of enormous therapeutic potential. It was used in formulations for skin ailments, digestive disorders, and detoxification.

Medical Uses: Antibacterial, antifungal, antiviral, skin care.
