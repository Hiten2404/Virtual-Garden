Lotus (Nelumbo nucifera)

The Lotus is a symbol of purity, enlightenment, and rebirth across many cultures. This aquatic perennial plant is native to Asia and Australia and is revered in religions like Hinduism and Buddhism. Its stunning pink or white flowers bloom above water and are known for their self-cleaning properties.
Plant Attributes & Uses

Scientific Classification

Family: Nelumbonaceae
Genus: Nelumbo
Species: N. nucifera

Global Spread

Found in India, China, Japan, Southeast Asia, and Australia.

Preferred Climate

Thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, in shallow ponds and lakes with full sun exposure.

Medicinal Uses

Used in Ayurvedic and traditional Chinese medicine for treating diarrhea, inflammation, skin conditions, and as a calming agent.

Garden Information

Hardiness: USDA Zones 4–10

Climate Zones: Tropical, Subtropical, Temperate

Exposure: Full Sun

Soil Type: Silty, clay-based pond soil (underwater)

Water Needs: High — grows best submerged in water
