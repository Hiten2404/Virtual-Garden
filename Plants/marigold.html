Marigold (Tagetes spp.)

Marigold is a bright, hardy annual known for its golden blooms and strong scent. Widely used in gardens, festivals, and traditional medicine, it symbolizes positivity, protection, and healing.
Plant Attributes & Uses

Scientific Classification

Family: Asteraceae
Genus: Tagetes
Species: Various (e.g., T. erecta, T. patula)

Global Spread

Native to the Americas, now cultivated globally, especially in South Asia and Africa.

Preferred Climate

Thrives in full sun and warm weather; tolerates heat well and grows quickly.

Medicinal Uses

Traditionally used for its anti-inflammatory, antiseptic, and wound-healing properties.

Traditional Uses

Used in garlands, religious ceremonies, and festivals like Dia de los Muertos and Diwali.

Cosmetic Benefits

Contains antioxidants and is used in creams and herbal treatments for skin clarity.
