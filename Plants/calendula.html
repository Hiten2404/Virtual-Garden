Calendula (Calendula officinalis)

Calendula, also known as pot marigold, is a bright, cheerful flower with medicinal and cosmetic properties. It is commonly used in herbal remedies, salves, and teas.
Plant Attributes & Uses

Scientific Classification

Family: Asteraceae
Genus: Calendula
Species: C. officinalis

Global Spread

Native to southern Europe but cultivated worldwide in temperate climates.

Preferred Climate

Prefers full sun and cool to warm climates. Tolerates mild frost.

Medicinal Uses

Used to treat skin irritation, inflammation, cuts, and rashes; promotes wound healing.

Traditional Uses

Infused in oils, made into teas, used in tinctures and ointments for natural healing.

Cosmetic Benefits

Commonly found in creams and lotions to soothe sensitive or dry skin.
