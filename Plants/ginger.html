Ginger (Zingiber officinale)

Ginger is a flowering plant widely valued for its spicy, aromatic rhizome. It has been used for centuries in cooking, traditional medicine, and natural wellness practices around the world.
Plant Attributes & Uses

Scientific Classification

Family: Zingiberaceae
Genus: Zingiber
Species: Z. officinale

Global Spread

Native to Southeast Asia; cultivated globally in warm and humid climates.

Preferred Climate

Thrives in tropical, subtropical environments with moist, well-drained soil.

Medicinal Uses

Used to treat nausea, inflammation, colds, indigestion, and muscle soreness.

Traditional Uses

Used in teas, pastes, tonics, and remedies for its warming and digestive properties.

Cosmetic Benefits

Ginger extract is found in products for stimulating circulation and toning skin.
