Clove (Syzygium aromaticum)

The clove plant is native to the Maluku Islands and has been treasured for thousands of years for its aromatic flower buds. It thrives in tropical climates and is widely cultivated for its medicinal and culinary uses. This plant has a significant cultural, historical, and spiritual presence around the world.
Plant Attributes & Uses

Scientific Classification

Family: Myrtaceae
Genus: Syzygium
Species: S. aromaticum

Global Spread

Cultivated in Madagascar, Zanzibar, Sri Lanka, India, and Brazil.

Preferred Climate

Humid tropical regions, 20–30°C, well-drained loamy soil.

History

Used in trade and medicine for over 2,000 years.

Cultural Significance

Used in incense, purification rituals, symbolic meanings.

Traditional Uses

Treats toothaches, nausea, colds; breath freshener and preservative.

Ayurvedic Benefits

Improves digestion, supports respiratory health, balances kapha and vata.

Medicinal Uses

Antibacterial, antifungal, analgesic, anesthetic; used in dentistry.

Active Components

Contains eugenol, tannins, flavonoids, volatile oils.

Modern Research

Studied for anti-inflammatory and antimicrobial properties.

Care Tips

Needs full sun, organic compost, and regular but moderate watering.

Garden Information

Hardiness: Zones 7–9
Heat Zones: 7–8
Climate Zones: Tropical, Subtropical
Exposure: Full Sun
Season of Interest: Year-round (flowers, buds)
Maintenance: Moderate
Water Needs: Regular, moist soil
Soil Type: Loam, well-drained
Soil pH: Neutral to slightly acidic
Soil Drainage: Well-drained
Tolerance: Heat, humidity
Attracts: Pollinators
Landscaping Use: Tropical gardens, spice gardens
