Aloe Vera (Aloe barbadensis miller)

Aloe Vera is a succulent plant species known for its thick, fleshy leaves containing a soothing gel. Widely used for its medicinal, cosmetic, and therapeutic properties.
Plant Attributes & Uses

Scientific Classification

Family: Asphodelaceae
Genus: Aloe
Species: A. barbadensis miller

Global Spread

Grown in tropical and subtropical regions worldwide.

Preferred Climate

Warm, dry climates, requires minimal water.

Medicinal Uses

Soothes burns, heals wounds, supports digestion.

Traditional Uses

Applied topically for skin care, consumed as juice.

Cosmetic Benefits

Hydrates skin, reduces acne, used in shampoos and lotions.
