Rosemary (Salvia rosmarinus)

Rosemary is a woody, perennial herb with fragrant, evergreen needle-like leaves. Known for its culinary and medicinal uses, it's cherished in Mediterranean cuisine and folk remedies alike.
Plant Attributes & Uses

Scientific Classification

Family: Lamiaceae
Genus: Salvia
Species: S. rosmarinus

Global Spread

Native to the Mediterranean region; widely grown in temperate climates globally.

Preferred Climate

Warm, sunny climates with well-drained soil; drought-tolerant once established.

Medicinal Uses

Supports memory, digestion, and circulation; used as a natural antioxidant and anti-inflammatory.

Traditional Uses

Used in smudging, herbal baths, teas, and as a symbol of remembrance in various cultures.

Cosmetic Benefits

Used in shampoos and toners to promote hair growth and skin clarity.
